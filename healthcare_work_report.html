Healthcare benchmarks (MedQA, IgakuQA): work summary and effort

Overview

Across May and early June 2026 we evaluated frontier models on two medical question-answering benchmarks: MedQA (US-style medical licensing questions in English) and IgakuQA (the Japanese Medical Licensing Examination, JMLE). The work split into two streams. For MedQA we ran a head-to-head of Claude Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5 and then audited the disagreements, which surfaced label-quality issues in the dataset rather than a clean capability gap. For IgakuQA we implemented the task from scratch as an Inspect evaluation, found and fixed a prompt bug affecting linked clinical cases, and compared the same two models, both of which score above 99%. A short follow-up in June added a DeepSeek run and smoke tests. Evaluation runs mostly executed unattended; the effort figures below count human working time.

Day-by-day log

MedQA (May 3, follow-up Jun 9)

May 3 · Two-model run and disagreement audit initial evaluation, analysis · ~2 d

Ran Claude Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5 over the 1,273-question 5-option English subset, then built a cross-model grid (both-correct, both-wrong, and the two disagreement cells) with a paired significance test. Hand-reviewed the cases where both models missed: most converged on the same defensible non-gold answer, pointing to label-quality issues rather than a capability gap. A sample-level fairness case study (cocaine-associated chest pain) compared the 4-option and 5-option dataset variants against current clinical guidelines.

Jun 9 · Smoke tests additional evaluation · ~0.5 d

A few small MedQA runs, likely model verification or diagnostics; minimal documentation.

IgakuQA (May 8, May 12, follow-up Jun 9)

May 8 · Task implementation, evaluation, and the linked-case fix setup, evaluation, debugging · ~3.5 d

Implemented the JMLE task from scratch: per-question dispatch between single-answer and multi-answer prompts (without leaking the answer count), a custom scorer with two committee-answer overrides, and handling for the exam's six sections. Ran both models on the 2022 exam and discovered that linked clinical-case stems were not being prepended to their sub-questions; fixed it, validated on the failed items, then re-ran both models in full. Documented the benchmark structure and a pre-fix vs post-fix failure analysis. This was the heaviest single day of the healthcare work.

May 12 · Implementation notes reporting · ~0.5 d

Wrote up two subtle implementation points (the single-vs-multi prompt dispatch and a committee-answer override) as a short explainer.

Jun 9 · DeepSeek run additional evaluation · ~0.5 d

Ran a DeepSeek model on the same 2022 slice for comparison: 97.0% accuracy, with one sample blocked by a content filter.

Deliverables

  • IgakuQA task implemented as an Inspect evaluation (prompt dispatch, custom scorer with committee overrides, multi-year multi-section dataset handling), with the linked-clinical-case fix.
  • MedQA two-model comparison: cross-model grid, paired significance test, and a hand-reviewed taxonomy of disagreements pointing to dataset label-quality issues.
  • MedQA sample-level fairness case study comparing 4-option and 5-option variants against clinical guidelines.
  • IgakuQA benchmark documentation (exam structure, scoring rules, schema) and a pre-fix vs post-fix failure analysis.
  • Implementation notes on the prompt-dispatch and committee-override logic.
  • Self-contained Inspect log viewers bundling the MedQA and IgakuQA evaluation runs.
