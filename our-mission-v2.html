01 — CONTEXT

A field at an inflection point.

Awareness of advanced AI — and of its safety risks — has risen sharply across the Japanese public and policy world. But national attention has concentrated almost entirely on integration: putting AI to beneficial use, rather than building the capacity to make it safe.

The result is a landscape with real momentum but a thin safety core. The people, institutions, and resources exist — they are simply scattered, and rarely pointed at frontier safety work.

  • GovernmentJapan's AI Safety Institute focuses on benchmarks and evaluations, allocating much of the work to research-consulting firms — strong on real-world business conditions, still building depth in safety research.
  • IndustryCompanies and startups are shipping AI products with an immediate need for technical, legal, and security measures — but little in-house safety context to draw on.
  • AcademiaResearch centers on AI for science. Awareness of frontline AI safety research remains low, with few channels into the international field.

02 — THE GAP

A world-class talent base — almost none of it aimed at AI safety.

Japan produces a deep, world-class engineering and research base. Yet global capacity in technical AI safety and governance remains critically limited, and virtually none of this top-percentile talent is funneled toward it.

The talent flows almost entirely into traditional roles — not for lack of ability, but because the context and infrastructure to apply those skills to AI safety simply do not yet exist locally. Where the field does exist, it is fragmented: capable people and organizations working in isolation, unaware of one another.

No on-ramp

The field operates almost entirely in English, with few entry points for Japanese-speaking practitioners.

No coordination

Government, industry, and academia rarely meet across the technical–policy divide.

No home

No shared institution exists to turn latent talent into sustained safety contribution.

“Talent, organizations, and resources exist in Japan — but scattered and disconnected. Our work is to weave them into a coherent community.”
The AI Safety Tokyo thesis

03 — WHAT WE DO

Two pillars: educate, and connect.

Since 2023 we have run a sustained, technically serious seminar series out of central Tokyo — over one hundred sessions, each built around a single paper, report, or policy question. Around that core, our work falls into two pillars.

Pillar I

Education

Building the on-ramps that let talented people learn the field — in both English and Japanese, from first exposure to research-level depth.

  • Monthly seminar series — paper-by-paper study sessions spanning interpretability, alignment, capabilities forecasting, and AI governance, conducted in English.
  • A technical AI safety accelerator — a structured training program bringing hands-on safety research to Tokyo.
  • A Japanese-language track — lowering the language barrier that keeps many domestic practitioners out of a field that runs in English.
  • Foundational courses — adapting international introductory programs for a Japan-based audience.
Pillar II

Connect

Weaving researchers, institutions, and decision-makers into one ecosystem — across the technical–policy divide, and across borders.

  • International conferences — co-organizing Tokyo's Technical AI Safety conference, connecting Japan-based researchers with leading groups abroad.
  • Government & policy — working with Japan's AI Safety Institute and the national network of AI alignment researchers.
  • Universities — helping build AI safety coursework, curricula, and research capacity at Japanese institutions.
  • Embassies & diplomacy — co-organizing AI safety forums and dialogues that bring policy, industry, and academic actors together.

04 — WHERE WE'RE GOING

From fragmentation to a coherent community.

The pieces are in place; what's missing is reach and connective tissue. To realize the thesis we need more scale and more linkages — united under one organization.

SCALE — EDUCATION

Run training at a steady cadence

Regular English and Japanese tracks, plus briefings and consulting tailored to the real needs of Japan's AI industry — research-consulting firms, practitioners, and academic working groups deploying AI that needs safety and security measures.

SCALE — RESEARCH

Convene the field

An annual Tokyo Technical AI Safety conference, sustained exchange between researchers in Japan and abroad, and hackathons in partnership with international research organizations.

LINKAGES — ECOSYSTEM

Build the connective tissue

Partnerships with embassies and chambers of commerce to host governance dialogues among ambassadors, policymakers, and business leaders; deeper ties with universities; and broader community building for newcomers.

LINKAGES — A HOME

Establish a co-working hub

A dedicated physical space for AI safety in Tokyo — modeled on established hubs abroad — to give the community a shared home where collaboration compounds.
